Scientific Foundation & Data Sources

Primary Standards

  • ISO 9920:2007: Ergonomics of the thermal environment — Estimation of thermal insulation and water vapour resistance of a clothing ensemble. Primary reference for clothing thermal insulation values (clo, Rcl) and evaporative resistance (Ret).
  • ISO 11092:2014: Textiles — Physiological effects — Measurement of thermal and water-vapour resistance under steady-state conditions. Defines standardized test methodology for measuring Rct and Ret using the "sweating guarded hotplate."
  • ASHRAE 55-2020: Thermal environmental conditions for human occupancy. Standard metabolic rates (METs) and comfort zones used for activity level calculations.

Thermal Comfort Models

  • Fanger, P. O. (1970): Thermal Comfort: Analysis and Applications in Environmental Engineering. Danish Technical Press. Foundational PMV/PPD model for thermal sensation prediction.

Fabric Properties & Testing Research

  • Li, Y. (2001): "The science of clothing comfort." Textile Progress, 31(1-2), 1-135. Comprehensive review of heat and moisture transfer through textiles, including comparative fabric data tables.
  • Havenith, G., et al. (2008): "Clothing evaporative resistance — Proposal for improved representation in standards and models." Ergonomics, 51(5), 660-673. Wet fabric performance and resistance increase factors.
  • McCullough, E. A., et al. (2003): "A comprehensive database for estimating clothing insulation." ASHRAE Transactions, 109(2). Extensive database of garment thermal and evaporative resistance values.
  • McCullough, E. A., & Wyon, D. P. (1983): "Insulation characteristics of winter and summer indoor clothing." ASHRAE Transactions, 89(2). Early comparative study including synthetic performance fabrics.

Technical Specifications

  • Invista (2019): COOLMAX® Technical Bulletin. Manufacturer specifications for 4-channel engineered polyester fiber moisture management properties.
  • Lenzing AG: TENCEL™ Lyocell Technical Data. Moisture regain, thermal, and comfort properties of cellulosic Lyocell fiber.

Data Transparency Note

All fabric resistance values (Rct, Ret) in this simulator represent typical single-layer fabric properties derived from published ranges in the peer-reviewed literature and standards cited above. These are not direct measurements from a proprietary database, but rather evidence-based representative values validated against ISO 9920:2007 reference tables and published research. Actual garment performance varies based on construction, thickness, weave/knit density, and fit. For precise applications, laboratory testing per ISO 11092:2014 is recommended.
